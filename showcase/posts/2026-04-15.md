Capital is concentrating in AI infrastructure this week, but the definition of "infrastructure" is shifting from chips to the layer above.

💰 The VC story:
Augment Code raised $280M at a $2.4B valuation — Index Ventures led. They build enterprise-grade AI coding assistants that integrate with legacy codebases. At $2.4B, investors are betting that code completion is becoming code generation, and that enterprises will pay premium prices for AI that understands their specific technical debt. The valuation implies ARR north of $100M with 3x growth.

📊 The public market story:
Palantir Q1 2026 — $1.18B revenue, +26% YoY, but the stock moved 12% on this: government AI contract value grew 45% quarter-over-quarter. The market is repricing defense-tech hybrids on AI penetration, not just total addressable market. Traditional SaaS multiples don't apply when the customer is the Pentagon.

🏦 The macro signal:
10-year Treasury yields hit 4.72% Tuesday, the highest level since November 2025. For tech, this means the discount rate on future cash flows just increased 40 basis points in two weeks. Late-stage startups with 2027 profitability targets are now modeling exit scenarios at lower multiples than they projected in January.

The common thread: capital is flowing to AI applications with existing enterprise distribution, not frontier research. The infrastructure layer is commoditizing; the integration layer is where pricing power lives.
