Capital is consolidating around AI infrastructure this week — not the models, but the picks and shovels that make them deployable at scale.

💰 The VC story:
Glean closed a $260M Series E at a $4.6B valuation, with Sequoia leading and IVP participating. They build enterprise AI search that connects to your existing SaaS stack. At $4.6B, investors are betting that "AI search" becomes the default interface for enterprise knowledge work within 24 months — not a feature in Slack or Notion, but a standalone layer. That is a crowded bet, but the revenue multiples suggest customers are renewing at rates that justify the price.

📊 The public market story:
CrowdStrike reported Q1 FY2027 revenue of $1.15B, up 30% year-over-year. The number that moved the stock 8% was this: AI-native Falcon platform modules now account for 40% of net new ARR. Security is becoming an AI-first category faster than expected — buyers are skipping traditional endpoint solutions and going straight to autonomous threat detection. The TAM just expanded, but so did the R&D required to compete.

🏦 The macro signal:
March PPI came in at +0.4% month-over-month versus +0.2% expected. Core PPI +2.8% YoY. The market is now pricing one Fed cut in 2026 instead of three. For tech: any infrastructure build-out modeled on cheap capital through 2027 needs to be stress-tested at 4.5% terminal rates. CFOs are already extending depreciation schedules.

The common thread: capital is paying premium prices only for AI with infrastructure characteristics — high switching costs, data network effects, and clear ROI metrics. Everything else is getting repriced downward.
